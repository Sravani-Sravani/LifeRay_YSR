About Us

Aarogyasri Healthcare scheme is a flagship program conceived and implemented in the erstwhile state of undivided Andhra Pradesh by the then chief minister Late Dr Y S Rajashekar Reddy garu in the year in the year 2009. Subsequently after bifurcation of the state, the program was renamed as Dr. YSR Aarogyasri Health Insurance Scheme and is being continued in the state of Andhra Pradesh.

With the vision to provide universal health coverage for all the sections of people in the state by providing cashless health care treatments and post operative sustenance facility to the need, the Program has directly impacted the lives of the people which were under distress earlier unable to meet the financial needs for their treatment. The scheme has grown rapidly from about 400 network hospitals and 800 procedures to over 2000 hospitals and 2446 procedures currently.

Beauty of the scheme is that people now can choose their choice of hospital for treatment from the empanelled 2000+ hospitals in both Government and corporate and without any financial burden

Core Objectives of the Scheme
  • To provide free, quality hospital care and equity of access to BPL families by provision of quality medical services from identified network of health care providers through a self-funded reimbursement mechanism (serviced by Trust).
  • To provide financial security against the catastrophic health expenditures.
  • To strengthen the Government Hospitals through demand side financing.
  • To provide complete health coverage for all the sections of the people belonging to the State of Andhra Pradesh.

Aarogyasri Healthcare Trust

To facilitate the effective implementation of the scheme, the State Government has set up Dr.YSR Aarogyasri Health Care Trust, under the chairmanship of the Honorable Chief Minister. The Trust is administered by a Chief Executive Officer who is an IAS Officer. The trust, in consultation with the specialists in the field of insurance and healthcare, runs the scheme.

The Trust being an independent body and single purpose unit is able to achieve universal health coverage to poor families in the state at a faster rate. Some of its activities could include governing, organizing and monitoring the scheme, capacity building of the stakeholders and negotiating with the providers.

Trust Functions
  • Empanelment
  • Medical Audit
  • Pre-Authorization
  • Telephonic Approvals
  • Claims Process
  • Capacity Building
  • Health camps
